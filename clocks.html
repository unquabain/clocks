Disk Clock

This is my favorite so far. You could build this with a motor and gears.

The top row is hours, middle is minutes, bottom is seconds.

The black bars (really a rotating disk) tells you a number. Add the numbers across as with the binary clock. They're arranged so that you can read the time to some desired accuracy without looking at all the bars.

Shift Clock

Shows you your progress through your work shift. Start and end times and multiple break markers can be set in the headers.

Digital Clock

What it sounds like.

Binary Clock

The little window is either white (0) or black (1). The top row is hours, middle is minutes, and bottom is seconds. The columns are 32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 1.

Vernier Clock

Works like the precision scale on Vernier calipers. You read the darkest part instead of hands. Inner ring is hours, then minutes, seconds, and fractions of a second in the outer ring.

Rhys' Clock

My 5-year-old son, Rhys, wanted to make a clock with me, so we built this one together.
